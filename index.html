The man who invented the World Wide Web
Tim John Berners-Lee is an Computer Scientist born in London, England.
He went to Sheen Mount Primary School, Emanuel School, and finally graduated from The Queen's College, Oxford in 1976.
Well, what is he known for, and why are you reading this?
That is because Tim Berners-Lee is celebrated as the inventor of the World Wide Web.
In 1989, CERN (the company Berners worked at) was the largest Internet node in Europe, so Berners-Lee decided to use this to his advantage.
Researchers all around the world needed a more efficient way to exchange data and information.
Because of this, he proposed taking the hypertext idea, and joining it to the TCP and DNS, and that's how the World Wide Web was created!
This new integration allowed users to navigate between documents easily, which created a universal space for information.
By 1991, the first website was live, which marked the dawn of the web.
His invention transformed the distribution of information as we know it.
It fundamentally changed how people access and share knowledge with eachother
It's literally how you're reading this webpage right now!
We can say one thing for certain: without this invention, technology, internet and even life wouldn't be what it is today.
