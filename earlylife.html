Early life of Tim-Berners-Lee
Tim Berners-Lee was born on June 8th, 1955, in London; his mother a mathematician and his father a computer scientist.
Both his father and mother were part of the team that worked on the first commercially available computer.
Because of the fact that he grew up in such a household with innovative minds, he was exposed to computing from a young age.
When he was a teenager, Berners developed an interest in tinkering with electronics (likely due to the nature of his home).
He spent a lot of his time disassembling and reassembling certain gadgets; he even built a basic computer using an old TV set!
Throughout his childhood, and through the works of his, he was able to grasp the fundamentals of computers and their software.
This layed the groundwork for the innovations of his later life.
After completing High school, Berners-Lee attended The Queen's College at Oxford University to study physics.
Although completing a degree in physics, he still continued to pursue his passion for Computer Science, even going as fair as to building his own computer with spare parts.
All of this truly showed just how deep he was in the path of Computer Science, and was a foreshadow of what he would become.
